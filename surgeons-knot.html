Surgeon's Knot

Difficulty: Easy
Strength: High

Best Used For

Line to LineLeader ConnectionQuick Tie

Easy knot to join two lines of moderately unequal size. Quick to tie and very reliable.

About the Surgeon's Knot

The Surgeon's Knot is the go-to choice when you need to quickly and reliably join two fishing lines, especially when they're of different diameters or materials. This knot is incredibly simple - essentially a double overhand knot using both lines - yet it's surprisingly strong and secure. It's particularly popular among fly fishers for attaching tippet to leader.

Best Line Types: Works with any combination - mono to mono, fluoro to mono, different line weights

Knot Strength: Retains approximately 90% of the weaker line's strength

When to Use: Perfect for joining lines of different diameters, adding tippet to leader, emergency line repairs, or when you need a quick, reliable connection in the field.

Speed Advantage: Can be tied in under 30 seconds, making it ideal for quick repairs or when fish are actively feeding.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Overlap the lines: Overlap the two lines by about 6 inches, with the ends pointing in opposite directions.
  2. Form a loop: Using both lines as one, form a simple overhand loop.
  3. Pass through once: Pass both tag ends through the loop once.
  4. Pass through again: Pass both tag ends through the loop a second time for extra security.
  5. Wet and tighten: Moisten the knot with water or saliva, then pull all four ends to tighten evenly.
  6. Trim excess: Cut both tag ends close to the knot.

Pro Tips & Troubleshooting

✓ Pro Tips

  • • Perfect for joining different line diameters
  • • Can be tied quickly even in poor conditions
  • • Works great with slippery fluorocarbon
  • • Pull all four ends evenly when tightening
  • • Ideal for emergency line repairs on the water

⚠ Common Mistakes

  • • Not overlapping lines enough (need 6+ inches)
  • • Pulling only two ends instead of all four
  • • Skipping the second pass through the loop
  • • Not wetting the knot before tightening
  • • Using with lines too different in diameter
