Fishing Knots

Master Essential Fishing Knots

Learn to tie the most important fishing knots with step-by-step instructions and video tutorials. From beginner-friendly clinch knots to advanced braid connections.

  • Improved Clinch Knot

    The most popular knot for attaching a hook, lure, or swivel to monofilament or fluorocarbon line. Reliable and easy to tie.

    Strength: High
    Purpose: Hook-to-line

  • Palomar Knot

    One of the strongest and most reliable fishing knots. Works with monofilament, fluorocarbon, and braided line.

    Difficulty: Easy
    Strength: Very High
    Purpose: Hook-to-line
    Learn to tie

  • Uni Knot

    A versatile knot that can be used for hooks, swivels, and joining two lines together. Also known as the Duncan Loop.

    Difficulty: Easy
    Strength: High
    Purpose: Hook-to-line
    Learn to tie

  • Blood Knot

    Classic knot for joining two lines of similar diameter. Popular among fly fishers for building tapered leaders.

    Difficulty: Medium
    Strength: High
    Purpose: Line-to-line
    Learn to tie

  • Surgeon's Knot

    A simple and quick knot for joining two lines, even of different diameters. Easier than the Blood Knot.

    Difficulty: Easy
    Strength: High
    Purpose: Line-to-line
    Learn to tie

  • FG Knot

    The strongest braid-to-leader connection. Creates a slim, smooth profile that casts beautifully through guides.

    Difficulty: Hard
    Strength: Very High
    Purpose: Braid-to-leader
    Learn to tie

  • Alberto Knot

    A modified Uni Knot for connecting braid to fluorocarbon or monofilament leader. Easier to tie than the FG Knot.

    Difficulty: Medium
    Strength: High
    Purpose: Braid-to-leader
    Learn to tie

  • Double Uni Knot

    Two Uni Knots tied back-to-back to join two lines. Works with both similar and different line types.

    Strength: High
    Purpose: Line-to-line

    Strength: High
    Purpose: Loop
    Learn to tie

    Creates a loop in the middle of a line for attaching a hook or dropper. Essential for multi-hook rigs.

    Strength: High
    Purpose: Loop

    Difficulty: Hard
    Strength: Very High

  • Snell Knot

    Strength: Very High
    Purpose: Hook-to-line

    Strength: High

    Strength: High
    Purpose: Loop

    Strength: High
    Purpose: Loop
