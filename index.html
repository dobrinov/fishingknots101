Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important fishing knots to learn?

The essential knots every angler should master are the Improved Clinch Knot (for hooks), Palomar Knot (for braided line), Surgeon's Knot (joining lines), and FG Knot (braid to leader connections). These four knots will cover 90% of your fishing situations.

How do I choose the right knot for my fishing situation?

Consider your line type (mono, fluoro, or braid), what you're connecting (hook, lure, or leader), and the fish species you're targeting. Our search and filter tools help you find the perfect knot based on difficulty, strength, and use case.

Why do some knots work better with braided line?

Braided line is slippery and lacks the stretch of monofilament, so it requires knots that grip well and won't slip. The Palomar Knot and Uni Knot are excellent choices for braid because they create multiple contact points and hold securely.

How can I improve my knot tying technique?

Practice with the video tutorials, wet your line before tightening (saliva works great), pull slowly and steadily, and always test your knots before fishing. Start with easier knots and gradually work up to more complex ones as your skills improve.

What's the difference between knot strength ratings?

Knot strength refers to how much of the line's original strength the knot retains. High-strength knots retain 85-95% of line strength, medium retain 70-85%, and low retain 60-70%. Choose stronger knots for larger fish and critical connections.
