FG Knot

Difficulty: Hard
Strength: High

Best Used For

Braid to FluoroBraid to MonoLeader ConnectionLine to Line

Very strong leader knot that joins braided line to fluorocarbon or monofilament leader. Preferred by many professional anglers.

About the FG Knot

The FG Knot is considered the gold standard for connecting braided mainline to fluorocarbon or monofilament leaders. This friction knot creates an incredibly strong, slim connection that easily passes through rod guides. While it requires practice to master, the FG knot's superior strength and low profile make it the preferred choice for serious anglers.

Best Line Types: Braided mainline to fluorocarbon or monofilament leader

Knot Strength: Retains 90-95% of the line's original strength

When to Use: Essential for finesse fishing, clear water conditions, and when you need a strong, low-profile leader connection. Perfect for bass fishing, saltwater applications, and any situation where leader stealth is crucial.

Why Professionals Love It: The slim profile passes through guides smoothly, the connection is incredibly strong, and it maintains excellent sensitivity between mainline and leader.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Note: The FG knot requires practice. Don't attempt this knot for the first time on the water!

  1. Prepare the lines: Hold the braided mainline and leader parallel, with about 12 inches of working length on each.
  2. Create the base: Pinch both lines together and begin wrapping the braid around the leader in a spiral pattern.
  3. Make wraps: Wrap the braid around the leader 15-20 times, keeping tension and spacing consistent.
  4. Reverse direction: Change direction and wrap back over the first set of wraps 10-15 times.
  5. Lock with half hitches: Tie 3-4 half hitches with the braid around the leader.
  6. Secure the leader: Tie 2-3 half hitches with the leader tag around the mainline.
  7. Wet and tighten: Moisten thoroughly and pull slowly to tighten, working the knot snug.
  8. Trim tags: Cut both tag ends close to the knot.

Pro Tips & Troubleshooting

✓ Pro Tips

  • • Practice at home before using on the water
  • • Use 15-30 lb leader with 10-20 lb braid for best results
  • • Keep consistent tension while wrapping
  • • Wet thoroughly before final tightening
  • • The knot should be smooth and cylindrical when done

⚠ Common Mistakes

  • • Not making enough wraps (causes slippage)
  • • Inconsistent wrap tension
  • • Forgetting the half hitches
  • • Pulling too hard too fast when tightening
  • • Using with mono-to-mono connections
